<p><ul><li>Negative Thermosensitive Hydrogel</li><li>Positive Thermosensitive Hydrogel</li></ul></p>
<p><p>The thermosensitive hydrogel market is divided into two primary types: negative and positive thermosensitive hydrogels. Negative thermosensitive hydrogels typically shrink in volume when the temperature rises, making them useful for drug delivery systems that respond to body temperature. In contrast, positive thermosensitive hydrogels swell upon heating, advantageous for applications like wound healing and tissue engineering, where increased hydration at elevated temperatures is beneficial. Both types offer unique advantages based on their thermal response characteristics, catering to diverse applications in biotechnology and pharmaceuticals.</p></p>
