<p><p>The newer-generation hemodynamic monitoring systems encompass several types, primarily categorized into minimally invasive and non-invasive systems. Minimally invasive hemodynamic monitoring systems involve the use of catheters or sensors inserted into the patient's body to provide accurate cardiovascular assessments while minimizing discomfort and risk. Non-invasive hemodynamic monitoring systems, on the other hand, utilize external devices such as sensors and monitors to assess hemodynamic parameters without penetrating the skin. Both types enhance patient care by delivering real-time data for better decision-making.</p></p>
